# Functions

## Linear Functions

**1)**

For each of the following, decide whether the function is linear\footnote{i.e., of the form
$y = ax + m$}. If it is linear, state the values of $a$ and $m$ respectively.

1. $y = 3x - 5$
1. $y = -2x$
1. $y = 4 - x$
1. $y = x^2 + 3$
1. $y = \frac{1}{2}x + 7$

**2)**

1. Write the equation of a linear function with slope $4$ and
y-intercept\footnote{Recall how $b$ \textit{displaces} the function
upwards/downwards along the \textbf{y-axis}.} $-3$.

1. Write the equation of a linear function with slope $-2$ that passes through
the point $(0,5)$.

1. Write the equation of a linear function with slope $\frac{3}{2}$ and
y-intercept $0$.

1. Find the equation of a linear function that passes through the points
$(0,-1)$ and $(2,3)$.

**3)**

Given the function $f(x) = 2x - 3$:

1. Find $f(0)$
1. Find $f(2)$
1. Find $f(-1)$
1. For which value of $x$ is $f(x) = 7$?

**4)**

For each function:

1. Create a table of values for at least three values of $x$.
1. Plot the graph in a coordinate system.
1. State the slope and the y-intercept.
    1. $y = x + 2$
    1. $y = -3x + 1$
    1. $y = \frac{1}{2}x - 4$

**5)**

1. Are the lines $y = 2x + 3$ and $y = 2x - 5$ parallel? Explain why.

1. Find the equation of a line parallel to $y = -4x + 1$ that passes through the point $(0,3)$.

1. Determine whether the lines $y = 3x + 1$ and $y = -3x + 1$ are parallel. Justify your answer.

**6)**

1. What is the slope of a line perpendicular to a line with slope $5$?

1. Find the equation of a line perpendicular to $y = 2x - 1$ that passes through the point $(0,4)$.

1. Show that the lines $y = \frac{1}{2}x + 3$ and $y = -2x + 1$ are perpendicular.

**7)**

A line passes through the points $(1,2)$ and $(3,6)$.

1. Find the slope of the line.
1. Determine the equation in the form $y = ax + m$.
1. What is the y-intercept?

**8)**

Consider the quadratic function $y = x^2 - 4$.

1. Calculate the values of $y$ for $x = -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3$.
1. Plot the graph.
1. What do you notice about the shape of the graph?

**9)**

Factorise the following quadratic expressions into the form $(x \pm a)(x \pm b)$:

1. $x^2 - 9$
1. $x^2 + 5x$
1. $x^2 - 7x$
1. $x^2 - 16$
1. $x^2 - 5x + 6$
1. $x^2 + 7x + 10$
1. $x^2 - x - 6$
1. $x^2 - 4x - 5$

**10)**

*Solve*\footnote{find $(x \pm a)(x \pm b)$ -- what does this tell us?} the
following equations by factorising:

1. $x^2 - 9 = 0$
1. $x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0$
1. $x^2 + 2x - 8 = 0$
1. $x^2 - x - 12 = 0$

## Quadratic (In-)Equalities

For exercises 1.--5., find a solution (i.e., the roots) to each equality in
$\mathbb{R}$. For 6.--10., **Bonus**, find a solution *set* in $\mathbb{R}$.

1. $-x^2 - 6x + 8    = 0$
1. $2x^2 + 5x - 3    = 0$
1. $3x^2 - 4x - 7    = 0$
1. $x^2 + 6x + 13    = 0$
1. $-5x^2 - 20x + 15 = 0$
1. $x^2 - 9x + 14 > 0$
1. $2x^2 - 3x \leq 2$
1. $x^2 + 4x + 3 \geq 0$
1. $3x^2 - 5x < -2$
1. $x^2 - 2x + 5 \leq 0$

**Bonus:**

Recall that $|x|$ is the *absolute value* of any expression $x$ which makes it
non-negative globally. E.g., $y=|x|$ simply makes $y$ non-negative whenever
$x<0$ (try plotting $y=|x|$ to \url{https://www.desmos.com/calculator}).

* $|2x^2 - 3x - 9| \leq 0$
* $|x^2-9| > 0$

**Question:** What does it mean, *visually*, to take an absolute value of a
              quadratic function, such as the ones above?

## Reciprocal Functions

**1)**

Consider the "basic" reciprocal function $f(x) = \frac{1}{x}$. Your task
is to:

1. state the equations of both asymptotes,
1. sketch the graph for $-5 \leq x \leq 5$, $x \neq 0$,
1. state the domain and image of $f$.

**2)**

Consider the function $g(x) = \frac{1}{x - 3}$. Start by plotting the graph for
$g$, then work out the following subtasks:

1. identify the vertical asymptote and explain how it differs from $f$,
1. state the horizontal asymptote,
1. find the $x$- and $y$-intercepts, or explain why they do not exist.

**3)**

Suppose functions $g_1(x) = \frac{1}{x} + 2$ and $g_2(x) = \frac{1}{x} - 2$.
Plot $g_1,g_2$ on the same axes and compare their asymptotes. Does a
solution $g_1(x) = g_2(x)$ exist? Argue graphically.

**4)**

Suppose a function $h(x) = \frac{c}{x}$ where $c$ is a constant in a fixed
interval, e.g., $c \in [-5,5]$. Explain (in plain words) what's the "effect"
of $c$ on $h(x)$. Focus on (i) $c < 0$, (ii) $c = 0$, and (iii) $c > 0$.

> Hint: Suppose a "helper" function $h^{\prime}(x) = \cfrac{1}{x}$, then $h(x) = c
> \cdot h^{\prime}(x)$. *Unsure?* Try
> [here](https://www.desmos.com/calculator/vkz5vqdxwn) and click the "play"
> button on $c$. \;)

**5)**

Consider a function of the form $f(x) = -\frac{2}{x^3} + 3$. Plot the graph and determine the domain and the image of $f$.

## Rational Functions

**1)**

Plot the graph of $f(x) = \cfrac{x + 1}{x - 2}$.

1. Find the vertical asymptote.
1. Find the horizontal asymptote by considering what happens as $x \to \pm\infty$.
1. Find the $x$- and $y$-intercepts.

**2)**

Determine the domain of $f,g,h$.

1. $f(x) = \cfrac{3}{x^2 - 4}$
1. $g(x) = \cfrac{x^2 - 1}{x - 1}$
1. $h(x) = \cfrac{5}{x^2 + 1}$

**4)**

Plot the following functions, mark their asymptotes, x-, y-intercepts, domain,
and image respectively.

1. $y = \cfrac{x-1}{x+2}$
2. $y = \cfrac{x^2}{x+1}$
3. $y = \cfrac{x-4}{x^2-1}$

**Additional question**: consider a function $y = \frac{x^2+3x+9}{x^2+6x+9}$.
You're asked to plot the graph. You could, of course, follow the "recipe" for
rational functions, but that will take some time. Is there a *shortcut*?
Explain.

**5)**

A municipality office is extending the train network with a new connection.
They consider different train models. They have that a particular train's
acceleration on the new track is modelled by a quantity $Q$ such that

$$Q(t) = \frac{100t}{t + 5}, \qquad t \geq 0$$,

where $t$ is time in seconds. Your task is now to:

1. find $Q(0)$, $Q(2)$ and interpret your answer in this context,
1. find the horizontal asymptote and interpret what it represents,
1. find the time at which $Q = 80$.
1. sketch the graph for $t \geq 0$, labelling the asymptote and any intercepts.

Now suppose that a competitor company offers a train model whose acceleration
is modelled by $Q^{\prime}(s) = Q(s^2)$, with $s \geq 0$. The municipality office
has to make a decision which train model to use. They want to have the fastest
train possible to cut commute time for residents on the track. Which train
model should be picked, i.e., is $Q$ or $Q^{\prime}$ a more suitable model?
Argue graphically.
